George Carlin

George Carlin is remembered for his unique and quintessentially New York voice, his coruscating mastery of the English language and his devastatingly funny insights into such timeless human follies as war, class, moral hypocrisy, God and golf.

George Carlin hosting the first episode of Saturday Night Live. Source:NBC

Here's timeline of George Carlin's Life

  • 1937 - Born in Manhattan, New York to Patrick John Carlin and Mary Carlin
  • 1957 - Discharged by the United States Air Force
  • 1959 - Temed up with Jack Burns and formed a comedy team
  • 1966 - Recorded his debut album, Take-Offs and Put-Ons
  • 1967 - Cast in Away We Go, a comedy show that aired on CBS.
  • 1972 - Carlin was arrested after performing this routine at Milwaukee's Summerfest and charged with violating obscenity law
  • 1981 - Carlin returned to the stage, releasing A Place for My Stuff and returning to HBO and New York City with the Carlin at Carnegie TV special
  • 1987 - Honoured with a star on Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • 1991 - Carlin had a major supporting role in the movie The Prince of Tides, which starred Nick Nolte and Barbra Streisand. He portrayed the gay neighbor of the main character's suicidal sister.
  • 1997 - Published Brain Droppings. It spent 18 weeks on the New Yok Times' best-seller list.
  • 1997 - Carlin was honored at the Aspen Comedy Festival with a retrospective, George Carlin: 40 Years of Comedy, hosted by Jon Stewart.
  • 2008 - Last stand up special "It's Bad For Ya!"
  • 2008 - Died at the age of 71
